# Basic Plumbing: Safe DIY Repairs

There is a clear line between what a homeowner should attempt and what requires a licensed plumber. Tap washers, toilet cistern parts, showerheads, and blocked drains are on the DIY side. Hot water system replacement, drain rerouting, and any work involving mains supply are not.

## Replacing a tap washer

A dripping tap almost always means a worn washer or O-ring.

Turn off the water at the isolation valve under the sink — or at the main if there's no isolator. Open the tap fully to drain residual pressure. Prise off the tap's decorative cap with a small screwdriver, remove the exposed screw, and lift off the handle.

Under the handle is the spindle. Unscrew it with an adjustable spanner (wrap the chrome in a cloth to avoid marring) and pull out the assembly. The washer sits at the base of the spindle, held by a small brass screw. Replace it with a like-for-like washer — take the old one to the hardware store if unsure.

Replace the O-ring on the spindle body while you're in there. A smear of silicone tap grease on the threads makes reassembly clean and helps the tap turn smoothly.

## Toilet cistern parts

A running cistern is almost always caused by a failed inlet valve or a warped flush washer.

Turn off the isolation valve behind the toilet. Flush to drain. Remove the cistern lid. The inlet valve (float mechanism) sits on the left; the flush valve (button or lever assembly) sits in the centre. Both are consumables and both are replaceable in 20 minutes with common parts from any hardware store.

If the seal between the cistern and pan leaks, replace the doughnut washer between them. This means unbolting the cistern, but it's still a homeowner job.

## Blocked drains

For a slow sink drain, try a plunger first. Fill the sink partially so the plunger has a water seal, then work it hard for 30 seconds. Repeat.

If plunging doesn't clear the blockage, try a hand-cranked drain snake through the trap. Undo the U-bend, feed the snake, and turn.

If a shower or floor drain won't clear with a snake, the blockage is deeper and you need a licensed plumber with a proper drain camera and rodding gear. Don't pour caustic drain cleaner into a fully blocked drain — the caustic will sit against pipe walls for hours and can damage older pipework.
