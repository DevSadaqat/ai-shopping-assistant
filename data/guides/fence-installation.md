# Fence Installation: Posts, Rails, Pickets

A fence is only as good as its posts. Concrete the posts properly and everything else is straightforward.

## Boundary check

Before digging, confirm the fence sits on your property, not the neighbour's. In most jurisdictions a shared boundary fence needs written agreement from the adjoining owner. Call the underground services locator before you dig — cutting a gas or comms line is expensive and, for gas, dangerous.

## Post spacing and hole depth

Standard picket-fence post spacing is 2.4m centres. Post holes should be one-third the total post height, minimum 600mm deep for a 1.8m fence, and at least 250mm across. In loose or sandy soil, dig deeper.

Dig with a post-hole shovel or a hired auger. Wider is better than narrower — a bit more concrete does no harm; too little is a fence that leans within a year.

## Setting posts

Drop a 100mm layer of drainage gravel into each hole. This stops the base of the post sitting in water.

Stand the post plumb using a post level (a special L-shaped bubble level that clips to two faces at once). Brace with two temporary timber stays screwed to the post and pegged to the ground. Mix concrete to a firm-porridge consistency and shovel into the hole, packing around the post as you go. Crown the concrete slightly above ground level so water sheds away.

Let concrete cure at least 24 hours before hanging any weight from the post.

## Rails

Cap rails (top and bottom) span between posts. Fix with galvanised bugle-head screws or coach bolts through mortises or metal brackets. Both rails need to be dead level along the run — set a string line from end post to end post as a reference.

## Pickets

Rip a spacer block from scrap timber to the width of your desired gap between pickets — usually 25mm. Butt each picket up to the spacer, then screw or nail through the picket into both rails.

Check plumb every fourth or fifth picket with a spirit level. Small errors compound quickly — a picket that's 2mm off vertical shows up as a visible lean by the end of the run.

## Finishing

Cap posts to shed water. Stain or oil the fence within a month of installation; treated pine turns silver-grey fast if left bare.
