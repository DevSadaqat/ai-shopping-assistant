# Bathroom Tiling: Floor and Wall

Tiling a bathroom is one of the highest-value DIY jobs — done well, it lasts thirty years. The two things that separate a durable job from a leaky, cracked one are waterproofing and adhesive selection.

## Waterproofing

Every wet area needs a waterproofing membrane applied before tiling. This is not optional and it is not something you can add later. Paint or roll the membrane onto the substrate, extending it at least 150mm up walls in shower zones and 30mm elsewhere. Allow the membrane to cure fully — usually 24 hours per coat, two coats minimum. Test coverage by taping a small piece of plastic to a suspect area overnight; condensation on the underside means the membrane isn't sealed.

## Substrate and layout

Cementitious backer board is the standard substrate for wet-area walls; plasterboard is not acceptable behind showers. Floors need a solid, level substrate — check for flex by walking the room, and stiffen the joists if there is any give.

Dry-lay tiles across the floor before applying adhesive. Start from the visual centre of the room and work outward so cuts fall at the edges, not the middle. If a wall is out of square, your visible cuts will show it — plan accordingly.

## Adhesive and setting

Use a modified thin-set adhesive rated for wet areas. Mix to a peanut-butter consistency — thick enough to hold ridges from a notched trowel, thin enough to slump slightly. Comb ridges in one direction with a 10mm notched trowel, then set the tile with a slight twisting motion to collapse the ridges into a solid bed.

Insert plastic spacers at every corner for consistent grout lines. 2mm spacers are standard for wall tiles, 3mm for floors. Level as you go — a tile that sits proud of its neighbours will show as a shadow line under downlights.

## Grouting

Wait 24 hours after tiling before grouting. Mix grout in small batches to a smooth, lump-free paste. Work it diagonally across joints with a rubber float, pressing firmly so the grout fills the full depth of the joint. Clean excess with a damp (not wet) sponge before it hazes — usually 15 to 30 minutes after application. A final buff with a dry cloth removes the last film.

Seal grout after full cure (7 days) with a penetrating sealer. Reapply every two years in shower areas.
